# Automorphic Form

[TOC]

## Define

> An automorphic form is a highly symmetric complex-valued function on an arithmetic quotient of a reductive group.

Automorphic forms are complex-valued functions satisfying invariance, growth, and eigenfunction conditions with respect to arithmetic subgroups or adelic quotients. They organize spectral and arithmetic information and lie at the heart of modern number theory.

## Properties

### Arithmetic Role

Automorphic forms package Fourier coefficients, Hecke eigenvalues, and spectral data into objects that interact with $L$-functions and Galois representations.
