# Measurable Space

[TOC]

## Define

$$
(E, \mathcal E)
\tag{Measurable Space}
$$

A measurable space is a set $E$ together with a [sigma algebra](./Sigma_Algebra.md) $\mathcal E$ on $E$. The set $E$ is the underlying space, and $\mathcal E$ specifies which subsets of $E$ are measurable.

Equivalently, a measurable space is the minimal structure needed to define measures, measurable functions, and random variables.

## Properties

### Basic Structure

- Since $\mathcal E$ is a $\sigma$-algebra, one always has
$$
\emptyset \in \mathcal E,
\qquad
E \in \mathcal E
$$

- If $(E, \mathcal E)$ is a measurable space and $\mathcal G \subseteq \mathcal E$ is also a $\sigma$-algebra on $E$, then $(E, \mathcal G)$ is another measurable space on the same underlying set.

- The intersection of any family of $\sigma$-algebras on $E$ is again a $\sigma$-algebra, so measurable structures can be generated from prescribed collections of sets.

### Generated Measurable Spaces

Given a family of subsets $\mathcal A \subseteq \mathcal P(E)$, the notation
$$
\sigma(\mathcal A)
$$
denotes the smallest $\sigma$-algebra containing $\mathcal A$. This is the measurable structure generated by $\mathcal A$.

This construction is fundamental in probability:

- Borel measurable spaces are generated by open sets.
- Product measurable spaces are generated by measurable rectangles.
- Filtrations are families of generated sub-$\sigma$-algebras.

### Typical Examples

- Finite or countable sets with the full power set $\mathcal P(E)$.
- $\mathbb R$ equipped with its [Borel sigma algebra](./Borel_Sigma_Algebra.md).
- Product spaces such as $\mathbb R^n$ with the product Borel structure.
