# Product Measure

[TOC]

## Define

Let $(X, \mathcal A, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathcal B, \nu)$ be [measure spaces](./Measure_Space.md). The product measure
$$
\mu \otimes \nu
$$
is the measure on the product measurable space $(X \times Y, \mathcal A \otimes \mathcal B)$ characterized by
$$
(\mu \otimes \nu)(A \times B) = \mu(A)\nu(B)
$$
for measurable rectangles $A \in \mathcal A$, $B \in \mathcal B$.

## Properties

### Product Sigma Algebra

The measurable structure $\mathcal A \otimes \mathcal B$ is the $\sigma$-algebra generated by measurable rectangles
$$
A \times B,
\qquad
A \in \mathcal A,\ B \in \mathcal B
$$

### Fundamental Role

Product measures are the correct measure-theoretic setting for:

- joint distributions
- independence
- iterated integration
- Fubini's theorem and Tonelli's theorem

### Probability Interpretation

If $\mu$ and $\nu$ are probability measures, then $\mu \otimes \nu$ is the law of two independent random objects with marginal laws $\mu$ and $\nu$.
