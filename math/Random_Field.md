# Random Field

[TOC]

## Define

> A random field is a stochastic process indexed by a multidimensional parameter set.

A **random field** is a family of random variables
$$
\{X_t\}_{t \in T}
$$
where the index set $T$ is typically multidimensional, such as $\mathbb R^d$ or $\mathbb Z^d$ with $d \ge 2$.

## Properties

### Covariance Structure

Second-order random fields are often described by
$$
K(s,t) = \operatorname{Cov}(X_s, X_t).
$$

### Examples

Examples include Gaussian random fields and spatial lattice fields.
