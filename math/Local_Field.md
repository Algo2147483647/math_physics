# Local Field

[TOC]

## Define

> A local field is a field complete with respect to a nontrivial discrete valuation and having finite residue field.

A **local field** is, in the arithmetic sense, a locally compact topological field with respect to a non-discrete topology. Standard examples include finite extensions of $\mathbb Q_p$ and the Laurent series fields $\mathbb F_q((t))$.

## Properties

### Examples

The basic characteristic-zero example is $\mathbb Q_p$, and the basic positive-characteristic example is $\mathbb F_q((t))$.

### Arithmetic Role

Local fields are the local building blocks attached to places of global fields.
