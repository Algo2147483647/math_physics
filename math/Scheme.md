# Scheme

[TOC]

## Define

> A scheme is a space obtained by gluing together spectra of commutative rings.

A **scheme** is a [topological space](./Topological_Space.md) together with a sheaf of rings, locally isomorphic to an affine scheme
$$
\operatorname{Spec} A
$$
for some commutative ring $A$.

Schemes generalize algebraic varieties and allow nilpotents and arithmetic base rings in algebraic geometry.

## Properties

### Affine Scheme

For a commutative ring $A$, the affine scheme $\operatorname{Spec} A$ has points corresponding to prime ideals of $A$.

### Gluing

General schemes are built by gluing affine schemes along compatible open subsets.
