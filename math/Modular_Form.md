# Modular Form

[TOC]

## Define

> A modular form is a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane satisfying a specific transformation law and growth condition.

For a congruence subgroup $\Gamma$ and weight $k$, a **modular form** is a holomorphic function
$$
f : \mathfrak H 	o \mathbb C
$$
satisfying the standard modular transformation law for $\Gamma$ and a holomorphy condition at the cusps.

## Properties

### Fourier Expansion

A modular form admits a $q$-expansion
$$
f(z)=\sum_{n\ge 0} a_n q^n,\qquad q=e^{2\pi i z}.
$$

### Arithmetic Role

Its Fourier coefficients often carry deep arithmetic information and produce $L$-functions and Galois representations.
