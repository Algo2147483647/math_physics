# Dirichlet Series

[TOC]

## Define

> A Dirichlet series is a complex-valued function given by a series of the form $\sum a_n n^{-s}$.

A **Dirichlet series** is a function of a complex variable $s$ of the form
$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} rac{a_n}{n^s},
$$
where $(a_n)$ is a sequence of complex coefficients. Such series are basic objects in analytic number theory.

## Properties

### Region of Convergence

A Dirichlet series typically converges absolutely in some half-plane
$$
\Re(s) > \sigma_0.
$$

### Arithmetic Role

Many arithmetic functions are encoded by Dirichlet series, which connect multiplicative structure with complex analysis.
