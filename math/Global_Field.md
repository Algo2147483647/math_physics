# Global Field

[TOC]

## Define

> A global field is either a number field or a function field of one variable over a finite field.

A **global field** is a field that belongs to one of the two standard arithmetic classes:

- a finite extension of $\mathbb Q$;
- a finite extension of $\mathbb F_q(t)$.

Global fields provide the common framework for many statements in algebraic and analytic number theory.

## Properties

### Two Families

The characteristic-zero global fields are number fields, while the positive-characteristic global fields are function fields of curves over finite fields.

### Arithmetic Role

Global fields admit completions at places, leading to local fields and local-global principles.
