## Problem

Geometric objects can be represented using different mathematical formulations.

| Representation            |         Expression          |
| :------------------------ | :-------------------------: |
| ***Implicit Equation***   |   $f(\boldsymbol x) = 0$    |
| ***Parametric Equation*** | $f: (u, v) \to \mathbb R^3$ |

### Uniform sampling on manifolds

Sampling methods are used to generate points that are evenly distributed on curves, surfaces, or higher-dimensional manifolds.

[Uniform sampling on manifolds](./uniform sampling on manifolds.md)

### Manifold Decomposition into Simplexes

Complex geometric shapes are often decomposed into **simple primitives (simplexes)** to enable numerical computation and rendering.

- 2D Polygon → Triangles: 
  - [Triangulating a simple polygon](./Triangulating a simple polygon.md)

- Implicit Surface in 3D → Triangle Mesh: 
  - [triangulation of point set in euclidean space](./triangulation of point set in euclidean space.md)


### Surface Reconstruction from Point Sets

Given a set of discrete points sampled from a surface, reconstruction algorithms generate a mesh representation.

- 2D Point Set $\to$ Triangle Mesh: [Delaunay Triangulation](Delaunay_Triangulation.md)



Solids Generated by Geometric Operations: **Solid of revolution**. In geometry, a solid of revolution is a solid figure obtained by rotating a plane figure around some straight line (the axis of revolution), which may not intersect the generatrix (except at its boundary). The surface created by this revolution and which bounds the solid is the surface of revolution.

**Prismatic Body**

If a planar region moves along a straight line in space, it sweeps out a **translational solid** (or prismatic body). The translation direction does not need to be parallel to the plane.

Many shapes cannot be represented using simple analytic equations. Instead, control-point based curves and surfaces are used.
